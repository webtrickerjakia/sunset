IS IT SAFE TO TRAVEL TO SOUTHERN AND EAST AFRICA?

Traveling to Southern and East Africa is safe and rewarding when guided by experts. We understand that safety is a top concern for travelers, and we take every precaution to ensure your well-being throughout your journey.

Sunsets in Africa meticulously plans each itinerary, considering the latest safety updates and local conditions. We have strong partnerships with trusted local providers who are well-versed in the region's nuances. Our team's firsthand knowledge and on-the-ground experience ensure that we only recommend destinations and activities that align with the highest safety standards.

We stay informed about any potential concerns and proactively adjust plans if necessary. Our aim is to provide you with a worry-free travel experience that allows you to fully immerse yourself in the beauty, culture, and wildlife of Southern and East Africa. Rest assured, when you travel with Sunsets in Africa, your safety is our utmost priority.

WHAT’S THE BEST TIME TO VISIT?

The best time to visit South and East Africa largely depends on the specific experiences you're seeking:

1. Wildlife Viewing: The winter months from May to September offer excellent wildlife viewing in many regions. Animals tend to gather around water sources, making them easier to spot. This is also a great time for birdwatching.

2. Great Migration: To witness the Great Migration in the Serengeti (Tanzania) and Maasai Mara (Kenya), plan your visit between July and September when herds move between these two areas.

3. Beach and Coastal Activities: For coastal and beach activities, the warm summer months from November to March are ideal. These months are also good for diving and snorkeling along the Indian Ocean coastline.

4. Landscape and Photography: The "Green Season" (November to April) is excellent for photographers, as the landscapes are lush and vibrant, and wildlife gives birth to their young.

5. Gorilla Trekking: If you're interested in gorilla trekking in countries like Uganda and Rwanda, the dry seasons from June to September and December to February are recommended.

Weather patterns can vary from year to year, and some parks or regions have different peak seasons. Regardless of when you choose to go, South and East Africa offer unique and unforgettable experiences year-round.

WHAT’S THE IDEAL LENGTH OF STAY?

The ideal length of stay in Africa can vary widely depending on the specific countries you plan to visit and the experiences you wish to have. For a comprehensive exploration of a single country or a specific region, a stay of 7 to 14 days can offer a well-rounded experience. This allows time for wildlife safaris, cultural encounters, and relaxation.

DO I NEED ANY VACCINATIONS OR HEALTH PRECAUTIONS?

Vaccinations like yellow fever, malaria prophylaxis, and other health precautions would be recommended, depending on the destination. Consult your healthcare provider or a travel clinic well in advance to ensure you're adequately protected.

WHAT'S THE LOCAL CURRENCY, AND CAN I USE CREDIT CARDS?

Local currencies differ across countries. While credit cards are widely accepted in urban areas, it's advisable to carry some local currency for rural or cash-based transactions. Inform your bank of your travel plans to prevent card issues.

WHAT ARE ENTRY AND VISA REQUIREMENTS FOR THE COUNTRIES I PLAN TO VISIT?

Entry and visa requirements vary depending on the specific countries you plan to visit in Africa. To ensure a smooth entry and stay, visit the official government websites or contact the embassies/consulates of the countries you'll be visiting. Keep your visa documents organized and accessible during your travels. Preparing ahead of time will help you navigate the entry and visa requirements seamlessly and enjoy your African journey to the fullest.
